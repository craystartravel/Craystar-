Common Questions

Frequently asked.

Does it cost more to book Carnival through Craystar?

No. Pricing is identical to Carnival.com. Carnival pays travel agencies a commission directly.

What is a CLIA credential?

Cruise Lines International Association (CLIA) is the industry-wide credentialing body for cruise travel professionals. CLIA-credentialed advisors have completed extensive training covering all major cruise lines.

Which Carnival ship is best for first-time cruisers?

For first-timers wanting the full modern experience: Mardi Gras, Celebration, or Jubilee (Excel-class). For first-timers wanting a more affordable shorter cruise: Vista-class or Dream-class ships, often departing from a drivable port.

How is Carnival different from other cruise lines?

Carnival is the casual, fun-first cruise line. Lower base fares, livelier atmosphere, more family-friendly chaos, and a younger guest demographic on average. Strong choice for first-time cruisers and family vacations.

Can you book group cruises on Carnival?

Yes. Group bookings (typically 8 staterooms or 16 passengers minimum) often qualify for special amenities, onboard credit, and reduced rates.

I have an existing Carnival booking. Can you take it over?

Possibly. Bookings can sometimes be transferred to a travel agent if booked within the last 60 days and not paid in full. There's no cost to transfer.
