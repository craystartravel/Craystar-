Common Questions

Frequently asked.

Does it cost more to book Viking through Craystar?

No. Pricing is identical to Viking.com. Viking pays travel agencies a commission directly.

What's the difference between Viking River and Viking Ocean?

Viking River cruises sail Europe's rivers on smaller longships (~190 guests) — more port-immersive, smaller-scale. Viking Ocean cruises sail saltwater destinations on larger ships (~930 guests) — more onboard amenities, broader destination range.

Is Viking really adults-only?

Yes. Viking accepts guests 18 and older only on all sailings (River, Ocean, and Expedition). No kids, no kids programs.

What's included in a Viking fare?

Substantial value. One shore excursion in every port, beer/wine/soft drinks at lunch and dinner, Wi-Fi, port charges, transportation between ship and airport, and gratuities on River sailings (Ocean gratuities are extra). Optional shore excursions and premium beverage packages are additional.

How far in advance should we book?

Earlier than mainstream cruises. 12-18 months out is standard for popular itineraries. Some sailings sell out 18-24 months ahead. Final payment is due 6 months before sailing on most Viking products.

Is Viking expensive compared to other cruises?

Per-night base fare yes, all-in cost often comparable. When you compare Viking's fare to mainstream cruises plus typical add-ons (drink package, Wi-Fi, gratuities, specialty dining, shore excursions), the difference narrows significantly.
