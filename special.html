Every Kind of Need

We plan for the whole spectrum.

Mobility, sensory, medical, cognitive, hearing, vision — we plan cruises across all of them, and often all at once for one family.

♿

Mobility

Wheelchairs, scooters, walkers, or limited walking. We match you to accessible cabins (roll-in shower, HC category), coordinate ECV/scooter rentals, and flag tender ports where accessibility may be limited.

🧩

Sensory

Autism, ADHD, sensory processing. Ship-by-ship recommendations — some Royal Caribbean ships carry an autism-friendly certification, and cabin placement away from high-traffic areas makes a real difference.

💊

Medical

Oxygen, CPAP, dialysis, insulin, severe allergies, feeding tubes. Equipment rentals delivered to your cabin through Special Needs at Sea, advance notification to the ship's medical team, and travel insurance review.

🌟

Cognitive & Developmental

Down syndrome, cognitive disabilities, developmental delays. We coordinate advance boarding where available, quieter dining times, and muster drill accommodations.

🦻

Hearing & Vision

Assistive listening devices, Braille menus, visual fire alarms in accessible cabins, ASL interpretation on select sailings, service animal welcomes. We ask the right questions of the Access Department before you sail.

🤝

Multi-Need Families

One child on the spectrum, another in a wheelchair, a parent on oxygen — real families have real complexity. We've planned cruises like this before, and we'll do it again.

How We Actually Help

We coordinate, advocate, and get it done.

Being a Certified Accessible Travel Advocate means we've been trained in the logistics that make or break an accessible cruise — and we have direct working access to Special Needs at Sea, the only equipment supplier approved to deliver to every major cruise line.

  • Accessible cabin selection — HC (fully accessible) vs. ambulatory-accessible, cabin placement, bathroom layout verification
  • Equipment coordination — scooter, wheelchair, oxygen, CPAP, shower chair, and hoist rentals delivered to your cabin
  • Access Department notification — every major line has a special needs desk; we contact them for you before sailing
  • Dietary accommodations — celiac, severe allergies, kosher, diabetic, kidney diets flagged to your head waiter in advance
  • Shore excursion vetting — accessible excursion options, tender port warnings, and port-by-port accessibility notes
  • Muster and boarding — priority boarding arrangements where available, accessible muster station assignment
  • Travel insurance review — pre-existing condition coverage, "cancel for any reason," and medical evacuation for high-medical-need travelers
